Group Contribution

We (the group) communicated through various channels; we used a Facebook group for most of the communication regarding meetings and big design-choices, a Facebook chat for most of the smaller design-choices and various chit-chat. We had a Google Drive map where we stored minutes from the meetings and images etc. We used Github to host the webpage as well as host individual code. We also had a Slack channel, which however was not used much at all.

We also had regular group-meetings, where we discussed design-choices and what to do next, as well as division of labour. Most meetings were attended by most group members, but as it is with any project involving several people with different schedules, it was rare that everyone was at the meeting.

The communication worked fine, the group seemed to pull in the same direction, and everyone had fairly similar ideas about what they wanted for the visualization. There were some redundant work when several group members worked on the same thing, but we used that to take the best bits of everyones work. Overall the communication was ok and the project flowed fairly seemlessly.

As for the division of labour, some people undoubtedly did more work than others, but everyone contributed in some way.
